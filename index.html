§1 — On the self

About

I'm a student at Hongik University, currently working as an undergraduate researcher at PEM Lab, where I study deep learning. But the question that actually keeps me up is older than any architecture: what is understanding, and could something built rather than born ever have it?

I read Wittgenstein for tying meaning to use rather than to some fixed inner picture, Derrida for what deconstruction does to any category we'd assumed was solid, and Nietzsche for his suspicion of easy certainties. Posthumanism is where these threads meet the machine: once "the human" stops being a stable category, so does the line we keep drawing around what gets to think.

§2 — Lines of inquiry

Research Interests

  • Philosophy of mind & AI what machine "understanding" would have to be for the word to mean anything
  • Alignment the gap between what a system optimizes for and what we meant
  • Language & meaning Wittgenstein-inflected — what "meaning" is when a system has no form of life
  • Deconstruction & posthumanism where the boundary around "human" stops holding, and what that does to how we build minds
